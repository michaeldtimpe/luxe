# Writing-agent eval — `gemma3:12b`

## paragraph-story (13.4s, 145 words)

**Prompt:** Write a complete story that fits in a single paragraph.

The rain tasted of ash the night Elara stole the cartographer’s maps, each parchment depicting a valley swallowed by the Mire decades prior—places her grandfather swore held the last blooms of the silverpetal flower. She traded a week’s worth of bread for the keys, the guard’s sleep a consequence of her grandmother’s potent chamomile tea, and pushed the cart toward the Whispering Hills, the wheels grating a mournful song on the cobblestones. The mire itself was a lung of fog and rot, tendrils reaching to clutch at her boots, but the maps, brittle and stained, guided her through the shifting landscapes, until, bathed in the sickly green luminescence of the swamp gas, she saw them – a single, shivering patch of silverpetals, their metallic sheen reflecting the despair of a lost world, and knew her grandfather’s stories, like the flowers themselves, were not entirely dead.

## characters (12.2s, 162 words)

**Prompt:** Give three unique character ideas for a novel. One short paragraph each. Make them distinct from one another in tone and setting.

Elara Volkov, a cartographer for the Imperial Geological Survey on the perpetually twilight planet of Xylos, meticulously documents the shifting, bioluminescent fungal plains, haunted by the phantom echoes of a vanished civilization and driven by the quiet desperation of a woman who believes her missing brother’s research holds the key to understanding – and escaping – their desolate world.

Reverend Silas Blackwood, proprietor of “Blackwood’s Curiosities” in the late 1880s New Orleans, is a man steeped in ritual and shadows, peddling oddities and dubious blessings to the city’s underbelly while secretly battling the creeping influence of a forgotten loa who sees the shop – and Silas himself – as a perfect vessel.

Seven-year-old Ren Sasaki, living amongst the crumbling, neon-drenched arcades and ramen stalls of Neo-Kyoto in 2077, possesses an uncanny ability to "hear" the digital ghosts clinging to discarded tech, a gift that draws the attention of both the Yakuza and a shadowy corporation seeking to weaponize the city’s collective memory.
