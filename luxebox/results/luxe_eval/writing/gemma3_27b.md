**Prompt:** Write a complete story that fits in a single paragraph.

The chipped Formica of the diner booth felt cold beneath Elsie’s elbows as she watched the November rain smear the neon of the all-night gas station across the highway. She’d been waiting for Leo since seven, nursing a lukewarm coffee and picking at a plate of congealed scrambled eggs. He’d promised, finally, to explain about the postcards – the ones arriving every Tuesday for the last six months, each depicting a different lighthouse on the Outer Banks, postmarked from towns he swore he hadn’t visited in years. Just as the waitress topped off her mug with that acrid diner brew, a figure materialized in the doorway, shaking rain from a worn canvas duffel. It wasn’t Leo. It was a woman with his eyes, and a faded anchor tattooed on her wrist, carrying a small, intricately carved wooden lighthouse, its beam inexplicably, softly glowing. Elsie knew, before the woman even spoke, that the explanation wouldn’t be about where Leo had been, but *who* he was.
